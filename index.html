Bacteriophage | Structural Biology

Welcome! I'm Jason, a structural biologist interested in how the evolutionary warfare between viruses and bacterial hosts.

I'm currently studying how bacteria have domesticated viruses in order to mediate horizontal gene transfer within a bacterial community. These domesticated viruses are known as gene transfer agents.

If you'd like to find out more about viruses that infect bacteria, please check out my research or check out my twitter

Moving to York!

I've recently moved to York to take up a postdoctoral research associate position in the groups of Dr. Paul Fogg and Prof. Fred Antson. I'm currently working on understanding how DNA packaging in domesticated viruses (gene transfer agents) is functionally distinct from viral DNA packaging, and how we can exploit these differences for biotechnological uses.

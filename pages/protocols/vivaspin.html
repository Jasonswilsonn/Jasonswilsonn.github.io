Vivaspin™ ultrafiltration spin columns are designed for fast, non-denaturing concentration and desalting of proteins and other biological samples.

  • Ease-of-use: One-step sample concentration in a single tube, for minimal sample handling and reduced sample loss, as well as pH compatibility over a broad range – pH 1 to 9.

  • Sample protection: Patented Vivaspin dead-stop technology ensures samples cannot be concentrated to dryness and enables direct concentrate recovery.

  • Ease-of-use: One-step sample concentration in a single tube, for minimal sample handling and reduced sample loss.

  • High flow rates: And minimal membrane blockage through the vertical polyethersulfone (PES) membrane design.

  • Minimal protein adsorption: Thanks to the Vivaspin™ polycarbonate tube design.

  • Wide selection: Vivaspin sample concentrators are suitable for sample volumes ranging from 100 μL to 20 mL, with a range of molecular weight cut-off (MWCO) values from Mr 3000 to 100 000.

Filtration

  1. Remove the concentrator cap.
  2. Use a pipette to apply a filling volume of buffer solution or deionized water into the concentrator.
  3. Replace the concentrator cap.
  4. Wash the buffer solution or deionized water through the membranes by centrifugation.
  5. If the pre-rinsed product is not used immediately: Cover the surface of the membrane with buffer solution or water and store the product in the refrigerator. The membranes must not dry out.

Diafiltration

  1. Apply up to 2 mL of sample into the concentrator. If the sample for diafiltration has a larger volume: Concentrate the sample to 2 mL and decant the filtrate.
  2. Insert a diafiltration cup into the concentrator and fill it with 10 mL buffer solution.
  3. Replace the concentrator cap.
  4. Insert the product into the centrifuge and centrifuge the product until diafiltration is complete.
  5. Remove the product from the centrifuge
  6. Remove the concentrator cap and the diafiltration cup.
  7. Recover the sample from dead stop pocket of the concentrator using a pipette.

Desalting

  1. Concentrate the sample to the desired level.
  2. Remove the concentrator cap.
  3. Discard the filtrate.
  4. Refill the concentrator with an appropriate exchange buffer.
  5. Concentrate the sample again.
  6. Repeat the process until the original buffer and | or contaminating microsolute has been sufficiently removed.
  7. If the desalting or buffer exchange is complete: Recover the sample.
